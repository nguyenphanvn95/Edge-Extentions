About Anki Tool

Anki Tool helps you create flashcards for Anki quickly and efficiently. Key features include:

  • Deck Selection: Choose the Anki deck where the flashcard will be added.
  • Card Type: Select a model (Basic, Cloze, etc.) for the card format.
  • Front & Back: Enter or edit content for the front and back of the flashcard.
  • AI Auto-fill: Automatically fill empty card fields using AI. Simply fill in one field and let AI generate content for the others. Limited to 10 uses per day.
  • Highlight Popup: Select any text on a webpage to see a floating popup with your card fields. Click a field to instantly send the selected text to it.
  • Add Audio: Upload and attach audio files to a card field for sound-enhanced learning.
  • Image Support: Copy and paste images directly into the text fields to enrich flashcards visually.
  • Highlight & Send: Select any text on a webpage, right-click, and send it directly to a field in the extension.
  • Cloze Cards: Hide parts of your text to test your memory. Select any text in a field, then click C1, C2, or C3 to create a deletion. Example: Select "Paris" in "Paris is the capital of France" and click C1 to create a card showing "[...] is the capital of France" on the front. Use different numbers to create separate cards, or use C+ for auto-numbering.

  • Card Preview: Preview how your cards will look in Anki before creating them. The preview shows both the front and back of your cards with the actual styling from your note type, so you can verify everything looks correct.

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Alt + G — Open the Anki Tool popup
  • Alt + B — Open the Anki Tool sidepanel
  • Alt + X — Close the Anki Tool sidepanel

To customize the keyboard shortcut for Anki Tool, please follow these steps:

  1. Open Chrome and navigate to chrome://extensions/shortcuts.
  2. Locate Anki Tool in the list of extensions.
  3. Click on the current shortcut for "Open the Anki Tool popup" and press your desired key combination.
  4. Ensure that the new shortcut does not conflict with existing shortcuts.
